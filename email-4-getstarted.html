The GhostClip Playbook

How to actually start
making money with this.

The system is set up. Now here's what to do with it.

GhostClip handles the production side. What you do with the clips is what separates people who build an audience from people who just have a folder full of video files. Here's the framework.

Step 1

Pick one platform and own it

TikTok, Instagram Reels, or YouTube Shorts — pick one to start. Don't spread yourself thin trying to be everywhere. Get 30 days of consistent posting under your belt on one platform before you expand. Volume beats perfection early on.

Step 2

Feed GhostClip content that converts

The best input for GhostClip is content with clear value moments — interviews, tutorials, podcasts, breakdowns, rants. Talking head content with strong opinions or practical advice clips the best. If your input is weak, the output will be weak. Garbage in, garbage out.

Step 3

Post at minimum 1x per day

This is where GhostClip pays for itself. A 30-minute video can produce 5–10 clips. That's a week of content from one recording session. Schedule them out, stay consistent, and let the algorithm reward you for volume. Most people can't keep up this pace manually — you can.

Step 4

Monetize the attention once it's there

Once you're pulling consistent views, the doors open fast. Brand deals, affiliate links, digital products, consulting, UGC work, community memberships — all of it starts with the audience. GhostClip's job is to build that audience faster than your competitors who are still cutting manually.

Step 5

Track what hits, double down on it

After 2–3 weeks of posting, look at your analytics. What topics are getting views? What hooks are stopping the scroll? Feed more of that type of content into GhostClip. The system learns your niche through you — the more intentional your input, the better your output performs.

You have the tool. Now it's on you to put the reps in.

Most people buy something, use it twice, and wonder why nothing happened. You're not most people — you chose GhostClip because you're serious. Prove it with consistency.
